The Problem

Every ride goes dark.

The moment a vulnerable patient gets into a transport vehicle, their family loses all visibility. Every competitor — Uber Health, Modivcare, RouteGenie — puts technology in the family's hands. Nobody puts it in the vehicle, facing the patient.

📞

"Where is my mom?" calls flood your dispatch

NEMT operators receive dozens of inbound calls daily from anxious family members. Each call pulls dispatch staff off live operations.

📋

No documented proof-of-service after the ride

Hospital discharge coordinators need a paper trail. Insurance billing requires it. Right now there's nothing — just a driver's word that the ride happened.

😟

Patients are invisible cargo

Elderly or cognitively impaired passengers have no way to reach their family, no reassurance about where they're going, and no emergency option that works.

How It Works

Set up in 20 minutes. Peace of mind all day.

01

Operator mounts the tablet

A ruggedized Samsung tablet locks into a custom headrest mount. Pre-configured, LTE-connected, MDM-managed. No IT team required. 20 minutes per vehicle.

02

Family gets a free app

The patient's family member downloads the CareRide app. They receive a live map, real-time video feed, driver credentials, and push alerts the moment pickup and arrival happen.

03

Patient sees their family, not a blank screen

The headrest tablet shows the patient their destination, ETA, driver name, and a live video thumbnail of the family member watching. One tap to call. One tap for emergency.
